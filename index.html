01 · Business Question

Does message personalization move the paywall?

Free-to-premium conversion is one of the highest-leverage levers for a streaming platform. Most paywall prompts are generic — the same message shown to every user regardless of what they have been watching, how deeply engaged they are, or what moment triggered the paywall. The hypothesis is that anchoring the upsell to a user's own behavior creates a more compelling reason to subscribe than a product-level value proposition.

This experiment tests whether a behaviorally personalized paywall modal converts at a higher rate — and faster — than the current static generic modal.

03 · Methodology

Analysis approach

Power analysis

A pre-experiment power analysis established a minimum of 1,774 users per group to detect a 3 percentage point MDE at 80% power and α = 0.05 (two-sided). The final dataset of 6,000 users per group comfortably exceeds this, providing ample power to detect effects smaller than the MDE.

Randomization & sanity checks

Randomization used stratified sampling — users were grouped by engagement tier (casual, regular, power) first, with a 50/50 split applied within each stratum. This was adopted after initial simple random assignment produced a borderline engagement tier imbalance (chi-square p = 0.049). Stratification guaranteed identical tier distributions by design. Pre-analysis sanity checks validated randomization integrity across four dimensions:

Statistical tests

Primary analysis: Two-proportion z-test comparing 30-day conversion rates between control and treatment, with a 95% confidence interval on the absolute difference.

Survival analysis: Kaplan-Meier survival curves built from scratch in Python (scipy / numpy) to model the probability of not yet converting over 30 days. Non-converting users are treated as censored at Day 30. A log-rank test determines whether the two curves are statistically different. This captures not just whether treatment converts more, but how much faster.

Subgroup analysis: Conversion rates compared by engagement tier and behavioral signal type using two-proportion z-tests within each segment.

06 · Recommendation

Decision & next steps

Ship to 100% of eligible users

The personalized paywall delivers a statistically and practically significant +7.35pp conversion lift (p < 0.0001), well above the pre-registered MDE of 3pp. Results are consistent across all engagement tiers and replicated across all sanity checks. The survival analysis confirms users convert 3 days faster under treatment, which also reduces the risk of users churning before converting.

Priority follow-up experiment

Binge behavior is the strongest-performing signal, suggesting users in active consumption moments are most receptive to the upsell. The natural next experiment is mid-session binge-triggered timing — showing the modal at the end of an episode when a binge pattern has been detected, rather than only when a paywall is hit. The hypothesis is that catching users at the peak of their consumption momentum will push the binge signal's conversion rate even higher.
